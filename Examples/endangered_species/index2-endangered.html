Why they're Endangered

Elephant population has been decreasing at an alarming rate over the past few decades, going from 100,000 in the wild at the turn of the century to an estimated 35,000-40,000. This puts them on the endangered species list. Their main loss of population is due to Ivory Hunting and competition for land with human-introduced animals like cattle. Elephants need a generous amount of land and humans often live very close to Elephant territory.
